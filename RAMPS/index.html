Robust Adaptive Multi-Step Predictive Shielding

Tanmay Ambadkar, Darshan Chudiwal, Greg Anderson, Abhinav Verma

RAMPS bridges the gap between formal safety and high-dimensional deep reinforcement learning. By learning a single global linear dynamics model—leveraging Deep Koopman Operators—and constructing adaptive multi-step robust control barrier functions, RAMPS accounts for accumulated model error and resolves high relative-degree traps. A minimally invasive quadratic program guarantees conditional forward invariance at each step.

The Limitation

Safe exploration at scale

Existing approaches force a compromise between mathematical rigidity and practical scale.

Formal Shielding
Offers strict mathematical certificates of safety, but hits a dimensionality wall around 10 states due to the cost of nonlinear propagation.
Statistical RL
Scales seamlessly to high dimensions, but enforces constraints only via penalties, permitting critical safety violations early in training.
The RAMPS Synthesis
Secures the mathematical rigor of formal shields while achieving the 348-dimensional scaling of statistical methods.

The Architecture

Three core components

Deep Koopman embedding

Transforms complex nonlinear control into a single global linear model. By mapping the environment into an infinite-dimensional lifted space via Deep Koopman Operators, powers of A precompute, enabling fast multi-step propagation without a patchwork of local approximations.

Resolving relative-degree traps

Standard shields fail on delayed-effect variables (where action shapes velocity, not position natively). By using an adaptive multi-step horizon, RAMPS secures control authority over these traps, keeping safety tight but never overly conservative.

Probabilistic certification

At every step, a warm-started quadratic program establishes model-relative forward invariance. It explicitly tightens constraints based on a data-driven error bound, yielding a mathematically certified safe action under half a millisecond.
